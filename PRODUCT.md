# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective and current UW–Madison students deciding whether to attend a Badger Journals meeting or join the club, plus current members checking meeting logistics (date, time, location) and RSVPing. Secondary audience: club officers managing meetings/submissions through `/admin`, a separate product-register surface.

## Product Purpose

Badger Journals is a student-run journal club at UW–Madison making cutting-edge research accessible to everyone. The public site's job is to make the club feel credible and worth joining, and to make attending a meeting frictionless — see what's happening, when, where, and RSVP in a couple of clicks. Success looks like a visitor understanding the club's value in seconds and converting to an RSVP or submission with no confusion about logistics.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly-credible and approachable-energetic at once: the confidence of a well-run academic journal, worn lightly with student-club warmth. Serious about the research, welcoming about the people. Voice is clear and direct, never stiff or corporate; typography and color should read "considered," not "flashy."

## Anti-references

No specific named anti-references given. General defaults apply: avoid generic SaaS/dashboard aesthetics on public-facing pages (this is a brand surface, not a product tool), avoid corporate-stock-photo academia clichés, avoid AI-slop scaffolding (gradient text, tiny tracked eyebrows, numbered section markers, identical card grids).

## Design Principles

- Credibility first: typography, spacing, and color should read as considered and trustworthy, not templated.
- Warmth without informality: approachable tone, but never juvenile or gimmicky — this represents real research.
- Logistics are the product: for meetings/events, clarity of date/time/location/RSVP beats decoration every time.
- One brand, two registers: public pages are brand-register (identity, credibility, conversion); `/admin` is product-register (task efficiency) and should not be forced into the same visual voice.
- UW Cardinal red (`--color-cardinal`, oklch primary) is the identity anchor already established in `global.css` — extend it, don't replace it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG 2.1 AA: sufficient color contrast, full keyboard navigability, visible focus states, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any motion. No additional stated requirements beyond that baseline.
